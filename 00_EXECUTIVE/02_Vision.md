## 1. One-sentence vision

> NEXUS is the operating system for AI agents — a browser that turns intent into outcomes.

## 2. The world we are building toward

In ten years, the dominant interaction model with software will not be "open a tab, type a URL, click a button." It will be "tell the system what you want accomplished, and it produces the result." The browser is the surface where this shift becomes tangible.

NEXUS's bet is that the browser — historically a passive document viewer — will be reborn as the **primary interface between humans, agents, and the internet**. The companies that own that interface will shape how the next decade of work gets done.

We are building NEXUS to be that company.

## 3. The shift we are designing for

### Today
- The browser is a passive document viewer.
- AI assistants sit in sidebars, bolted on.
- Humans perform every step: navigate, type, click, copy, paste, repeat.
- Productivity means "use many tools, manually."

### Tomorrow
- The browser is an **active operator** that executes tasks on behalf of the user.
- AI agents are first-class citizens, with tools, memory, and authority.
- Humans describe outcomes; agents produce them.
- Productivity means "describe the outcome, watch it happen."

NEXUS sits at the center of this transition. Not as an "AI feature" added to a browser — but as a **platform designed around the assumption that intent is the primary input**.

## 4. The three-layer vision

NEXUS is not just a browser. It is three things at once, and we hold all three to the same standard.

### Layer 1 — The Product (what users touch)
A browser whose home screen is not a search box but a single prompt:

> *"What do you want accomplished?"*

Every interaction is in service of answering that question. Tabs are replaced by **Workspaces** organized by goal. Sidebars hold persistent AI collaborators. The Cloud Browser Fleet runs in the background, executing scheduled and parallel work the user never has to watch.

### Layer 2 — The Platform (what runs underneath)
A multi-tenant, AI-native execution environment with:
- An **agent runtime** capable of spawning, monitoring, and supervising thousands of concurrent agents.
- A **memory engine** that retains user preferences, project state, and historical actions across years.
- A **model gateway** that routes each task to the right model — local or cloud, cheap or premium.
- A **browser automation layer** that can drive Chromium-based containers with state-of-the-art reliability.
- An **integration fabric** that connects to email, calendar, CRMs, code repositories, design tools, and cloud APIs.
- A **marketplace** where users install specialized agents and creators monetize them.

### Layer 3 — The Company (what we are)
NEXUS is an **AI software company that happens to have one human as founder**. The internal organization — engineering, research, product, design, QA, security, marketing, finance — is staffed by AI agents coordinated by a CEO Agent and overseen by the human founder. Every document in this repository is intended to be executable by an AI organization as readily as readable by a human one.

## 5. What success looks like at three horizons

### Horizon 1 — The capable browser (years 0–2)
NEXUS is a fully-featured Chromium-based browser with a deeply integrated AI workspace. Users log in, describe a task, and NEXUS either does it or shows them how. Power users install agents from the marketplace. Cloud Browsers become a paid differentiator. We are a credible alternative to Chrome and Arc.

**Measure:** 1 million monthly active users, 100,000 paid subscribers.

### Horizon 2 — The agent platform (years 2–5)
NEXUS is the default environment for AI-driven work. Solo founders run entire businesses on it. Small teams replace 5–10 SaaS tools with NEXUS Workspaces. The marketplace has thousands of agents, workflows, and integrations. Developers build on NEXUS the way they build on AWS.

**Measure:** 10 million monthly active users, 1 million paid workspaces, $100M ARR.

### Horizon 3 — The operating system (years 5–10)
NEXUS is the way humans and AI work together on the web. The browser is no longer the metaphor; NEXUS is the platform that hosts agents, automations, and applications — with web rendering as one of many capabilities. Devices from phones to AR glasses run NEXUS. Agents built on NEXUS exceed the capability of any single AI model.

**Measure:** 100 million monthly active users, 10 million paid workspaces, $1B+ ARR.

## 6. What we are NOT building

Equally important is what we refuse to be:

- **Not a Chrome clone.** Chrome-shaped thinking produces Chrome-shaped products.
- **Not an AI sidebar.** Bolt-on AI is the wrong abstraction.
- **Not a no-code tool.** Visual workflows are an interface to power, not a ceiling on it.
- **Not an LLM wrapper.** We do not depend on any single model's continued dominance.
- **Not a productivity suite.** We are not building email, calendar, or docs as standalone products. We integrate with them.
- **Not a chatbot.** Conversation is one interface to NEXUS, not the only one.

## 7. The user promise

For every user who opens NEXUS, the promise is the same:

> *"If you can describe what you want accomplished, NEXUS will get you closer to it than any tool you've used before."*

That promise must hold for:
- A 12-year-old doing homework.
- A solo founder running an e-commerce store.
- A security researcher hunting scams.
- An enterprise team replacing five SaaS tools.
- A retired user planning a vacation.

If we cannot keep the promise for a given user, we have failed — regardless of what other users we serve.

## 8. The founder's view

NEXUS is being built because the next generation of software will not be organized around applications. It will be organized around **outcomes**. The browser is the most universal application interface that has ever existed, and it is the right place to start that reorganization.

This vision is ambitious. It is also, in our view, inevitable. Our job is to be the team that gets there first and gets there right.

## 9. Failure modes we are guarding against

A vision this large can die in predictable ways. We name them here so we can recognize them early.

1. **The Chrome clone trap.** We ship a polished browser that nobody switches to because it looks like Chrome.
2. **The bolt-on AI trap.** We ship a sidebar that demonstrates capability but does not change behavior.
3. **The vendor dependency trap.** We build a product that dies if OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google changes pricing or policies.
4. **The complexity trap.** The product becomes powerful but unusable.
5. **The single-model trap.** We architect around one AI provider and lose portability.
6. **The marketplace graveyard.** We launch a marketplace with too few agents to feel alive.
7. **The privacy backlash.** A single high-profile breach ends user trust.
8. **The scope trap.** We try to ship Horizons 1, 2, and 3 at once and ship none of them.
