## 1. Mission statement (one sentence)

> Make every person with a web browser capable of operating an AI-driven team that accomplishes meaningful work on their behalf.

## 2. Mission statement (one paragraph)

NEXUS exists to make AI-powered execution accessible to anyone with intent. We believe that describing what you want accomplished should be enough — that the gap between an idea and a finished outcome should be filled by AI agents, not by human labor navigating dozens of disconnected tools. We build the browser that is also the workspace, the agent runtime, the memory store, and the integration platform. Our mission is measured not by features shipped, but by tasks completed, businesses run, and lives improved by users who would otherwise never have had access to this capability.

## 3. The guiding question

If a single question had to summarize NEXUS's reason for existing, it is:

> **"What do you want accomplished?"**

Every product decision is downstream of this question. The home screen asks it. The command bar interprets it. The agent orchestrator decomposes it. The marketplace extends it. The cloud browser executes it. The success metric answers it.

## 4. What the mission requires

To deliver on the mission, NEXUS must:

1. **Lower the cost of intent.** A user should be able to go from "I want to…" to "NEXUS is working on it" in under five seconds.
2. **Raise the ceiling of capability.** The hardest tasks a user can imagine — building a company, researching a field, running investigations — should be within reach.
3. **Preserve human authority.** Sensitive actions require explicit human approval. The user is always in control.
4. **Respect user data.** Information entrusted to NEXUS is the user's property, encrypted and never sold.
5. **Be dependable.** A browser that breaks is unusable. Reliability is a first-class feature.
6. **Earn trust gradually.** Permissions expand as the user proves comfort, not as we assume it.
7. **Stay open.** An open extension model, an open agent SDK, and an open marketplace are part of the contract.

## 5. What the mission forbids

In pursuit of the mission, NEXUS will NOT:

1. **Trick users into over-permissioning.** Permission prompts are honest. We do not dark-pattern users into giving up control.
2. **Hide agent actions.** Every agent action is logged, explainable, and reversible when possible.
3. **Sell user data.** Revenue comes from subscriptions and marketplace commissions, not from surveillance.
4. **Train foundation models on user content** without explicit, granular, revocable consent.
5. **Optimize for engagement metrics** at the expense of user outcomes. We measure outcomes, not minutes-spent.
6. **Ship features that are technically impressive but harm trust.** A clever feature that erodes confidence is not progress.
7. **Pretend the AI is human.** NEXUS discloses when actions are performed by an agent.

## 7. Mission vs. Vision

The distinction:

- **Vision (NX-DOC-0002)** describes the future we are working toward — the world we want to exist in 10 years.
- **Mission (this document)** describes what NEXUS does, today and every day, to bring that future about.

The vision can stay constant for years. The mission may evolve as the company grows, but the guiding question — *"What do you want accomplished?"* — does not change.

## 8. The mission in one line for the team

> **Turn intent into outcomes.**

When in doubt about a design decision, a hiring decision, or a partnership decision, the test is the same: does this help us turn intent into outcomes? If yes, do it. If no, decline it.
